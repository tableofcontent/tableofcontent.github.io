It's fun to be passionate!

My father has always taught me to put my heart into everything that I do. Otherwise, he would say it’s “not fun.” He even taught me how to be passionate with things as trivial as sweeping the floor, washing the dishes, or playing ping pong. As a result, I have learned to cherish all tasks and activities to be of equal value. Just because one handles big task doesn’t mean that those who handles small tasks matter less. All tasks are equal, it’s the amount of love, care and passion that is not.

Objectivity is rule number 1

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

I am passionate in what I do. But passion is like fire. It’s a double-edged sword which needs to be handled wisely. I have failed much in life. There had been too many times where my raging passion has been smothered to naught, and in some cases even left scars. How does one get back up once all that passion and energy has gone to waste? This is an important question, one that reveals resilience, courage, strength and the tenacity of a man—a true test of character. To me, I have found my answer to be as stated above: Objectivity. Passion needs objectivity. To put it generally: You can desire anything from reality, but what you desire from reality is mutually exclusive from what the reality is. It hurts most when reality is not like what you desire it to be. So to navigate this, one needs to take it to heart the fact that reality does not move according to one’s will. Rather, it moves according to the absolute law of cause and effect. Just because you worked hard and had given your all to be successful doesn’t always mean the reality will turn out as such. It’s important to stay with reality and never to put your heart a head of it.
